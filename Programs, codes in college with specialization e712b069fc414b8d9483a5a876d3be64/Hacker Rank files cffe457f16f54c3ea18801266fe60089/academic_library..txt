The working of libraries varies depending on their type and purpose, but here's a general overview of how different types of libraries typically operate:

Public Library:
Public libraries serve the general population and provide a wide range of resources and services.
They offer borrowing privileges for books, magazines, DVDs, and sometimes even digital media.
Public libraries often host community events, workshops, and educational programs for people of all ages.
Librarians in public libraries assist patrons with finding information, conducting research, and using library resources effectively.
Some public libraries also offer computer and internet access for those who may not have them at home.
Academic Library:
Academic libraries support the teaching, learning, and research needs of students, faculty, and staff at colleges, universities, and research institutions.
They provide access to scholarly materials such as academic journals, textbooks, research papers, and databases.
Academic librarians collaborate with faculty to develop collections that support the curriculum and research interests of the institution.
Academic libraries often offer specialized services such as citation assistance, information literacy instruction, and research consultations.
School Library:
School libraries serve students and teachers in primary and secondary schools.
They provide access to age-appropriate books, educational materials, and resources that support the school curriculum.
School librarians collaborate with teachers to integrate library resources into lesson plans and promote reading and literacy among students.
School libraries may offer storytime sessions, book clubs, and other programs to encourage a love of reading.
Special Library:
Special libraries serve specific organizations, businesses, or industries and provide information tailored to their needs.
They may focus on areas such as law, medicine, engineering, corporate information, or government documents.
Special librarians work closely with stakeholders to understand their information needs and provide relevant resources and services.
Special libraries may offer specialized databases, research support, and knowledge management services.
National Library:
National libraries serve as repositories of a country's published material and cultural heritage.
They collect, preserve, and provide access to books, manuscripts, maps, photographs, and other materials of national significance.
National libraries often have legal deposit requirements, where publishers are required to submit copies of their publications to the library.
They may offer exhibitions, public lectures, and educational programs to promote national literacy and cultural heritage.
These are just general overviews, and the specific workings of each library can vary based on factors such as size, budget, staffing, and community needs.
